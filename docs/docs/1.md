# Strategic & Initiation Documentation

Your previous inventory captured the essential, traditional documents for strategic project initiation. However, my recent research has uncovered a broader set of modern artifacts that are crucial for alignment, validation, and decision-making in today's software projects, particularly those in product-led and Lean-Agile environments.

These new artifacts aren't just nice-to-haves; they serve specific strategic purposes. PR/FAQ and North Star Metric documents align entire organizations around a shared outcome, while tools like the Opportunity Canvas, Riskiest Assumption Tests (RATs), and decision logs focus on systematically validating ideas and ensuring critical decisions are understood and linked back to business goals.

The updated inventory below integrates these modern practices. To use it effectively, first consider your project's methodology and maturity. Startups and early-stage internal tools benefit most from validation artifacts like Lean Canvas and RATs, while established, cross-functional initiatives require the governance of DACI logs or the PM² Canvas. The goal is a lightweight dashboard of living documents that drive action, not a bureaucratic checklist.

## 📌 Added Artifacts for Strategic & Initiation

### Methodological Frameworks (Structuring Strategic Work)

- **PM² Programme Canvas** – A collaborative tool from the European Commission's PM² methodology for defining key programme elements. This is ideal for large, multi-project initiatives, ensuring alignment on objectives, benefits, and strategic priorities.
- **Working Backwards Process** – A product development framework pioneered by Amazon that starts with the desired customer outcome and reverse-engineers the path to achieve it.

### Core Strategy Artifacts (Defining Why & Where)

- **PR/FAQ (Press Release / Frequently Asked Questions)** – A narrative document written from the customer's perspective, imagining the product is already launched. It includes a one-page press release defining the problem and solution, FAQs for customers, and an internal FAQ addressing feasibility. It serves as a strategic tool to align teams, evaluate ideas, and ensure market viability before writing code.
- **Lean Canvas** – Created by Ash Maurya, this one-page business template deconstructs an idea into nine key assumptions, including problem, solution, unique value proposition, and unfair advantage. It is particularly focused on identifying and testing business model hypotheses.
- **Value Proposition Canvas** – A strategic tool designed to align a product's features with customer needs by mapping the customer profile (jobs to be done, pains, gains) to the value proposition (products & services, pain relievers, gain creators). It is often used to validate problem-solution fit before detailed development.
- **Opportunity Canvas** – A concise, structured tool (one page) used to evaluate new product features or initiatives. It forces teams to align on the specific problem, target audience, proposed solution, and expected business impact before committing resources.
- **Strategic Initiative Alignment Canvas** – A structured framework designed to translate high-level strategic goals into specific, actionable initiatives. It helps ensure that projects are directly linked to business strategy.
- **North Star Metric (NSM) Framework** – A documented strategy that identifies a single, core metric capturing the essential value the product delivers to customers. The NSM serves as a decision-making compass across all teams. This document should also include leading indicators that drive the NSM.
- **Project Initiation Request** – A proposal document providing a high-level overview of the current situation (needs, problems, opportunities), desired outcomes, and initial estimates for effort, impact, risks, constraints, and assumptions.

### Validation & Assumption Artifacts (Testing Ideas Before Building)

- **Riskiest Assumption Test (RAT)** – A documented experiment designed to validate the single most critical and uncertain assumption underlying a project. The RAT defines a hypothesis and the minimal test required to prove or disprove it before significant development, creating a continuous improvement loop.
- **Assumption Log** – A document capturing all initial assumptions and constraints identified at project initiation. It is reviewed and updated regularly, as unvalidated assumptions represent key risk factors.
- **Hypothesis Template** – A structured format for stating testable predictions linking a solution to a desired business outcome. Used to frame experiments, features, or product backlog items, shifting teams from building "requirements" to validating hypotheses.

### Decision & Governance Artifacts (Managing How Decisions Get Made)

- **Architectural Decision Record (ADR)** – An append-only log documenting key architectural decisions, including the context, considered alternatives, decision outcome, and implications. It preserves the "why" behind system structure, clarifying trade-offs for future reviews.
- **Decision Log (DACI Framework)** – A record that uses the DACI (Driver, Approver, Contributor, Informed) model to assign clear roles and accountability for strategic project decisions. It prevents decision paralysis by specifying who has final authority and who must provide input.

## 💎 Modern Recommendations for Implementation

Updating your documentation practice requires a strategic shift. Here's how to bring these new artifacts to life effectively.

- **Start with validation before heavy documentation**: In uncertain or early-stage projects, prioritize lightweight validation artifacts over detailed specifications. Begin by documenting your riskiest assumptions and using a **Hypothesis Template** to frame an experiment. The **RAT** then becomes your guide to either pivot or proceed before significant investment.
- **Use Narrative for Strategic Alignment**: For ideas involving multiple teams or significant investment, use the **PR/FAQ process** to build alignment before writing a single line of code. The fictional press release forces customer-centric thinking, and the FAQ anticipates tough internal questions, reducing later friction.
- **Make documentation a decision dashboard, not a shelf**: Ensure every strategic artifact is a living document linked to a decision or a test. For instance, store **ADRs** in your code repository, treat your **Assumption Log** as a `to-validate` list for the product trio, and ensure your **North Star Metric** framework is accessible, not hidden away.
- **Contextualize your artifact selection**: Tailor your documentation suite to your project's context.
  - *For a startup's new product idea*: Focus on Lean Canvas as your one-page business model, RATs as your validation engine, and a Hypothesis Template to frame early experiments.
  - *For an established enterprise feature*: A PR/FAQ helps align cross-functional teams, ADRs capture significant architectural choices, and a DACI log clarifies governance for the release.
- **Do not forget ethical and strategic oversight**: For initiatives likely to have significant user impact, ensure your **Strategic Initiative Alignment Canvas** includes a field for ethical considerations and potential negative externalities.

By integrating these modern artifacts as strategic tools for validation and decision-making, you move beyond documentation for its own sake. You build a collaborative system that aligns your team, reduces risk early, and ensures every artifact drives real project outcomes.
